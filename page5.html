 Research

Research shows that literacy learning difficulties has no mark on a students' level of intelligence, yet the impact on a child’s self-esteem, motivation and achievement can be long lasting. Successful and effective literacy and mathematics intervention occur when students with learning difficulties/disabilities are provided with opportunities to engage in instructional support when teachers:

- explicitly teach skills and strategies rather than relying on incidental learning
- teach small amounts of material and emphasise key points to address problems with attention
- teach to mastery and provide cumulative review of skills previously mastered to aid memory
- teach the academic vocabulary required for each task to support language development
- teach skills in various contexts to assist students to generalise their knowledge

Source: NSW Department of Education (2017). Smart teaching strategies. Supporting students with difficulties in learning. https://education.nsw.gov.au/smart-teaching-strategies/numeracy/numeracy-overview/difficulties-in- learning

 Language Arts
The Literacy Empowerment Approach

Take a moment to answer this question…
How did you learn to spell, write and read?

Most children learn to speak the English language by being immersed in it as part of their everyday lives, as spoken language ability is prewired in the human brain; we are all born ready to talk. However, reading and writing skills are not prewired in the human mind, and need to be explicitly taught.
With more than 150,000 English words currently in use today, memorisation, and rote learning frequently present challenges for many students.

This is where we come in – We take the ‘guessing’ out of spelling, reading and writing, enabling students’ to feel empowered!
The Literacy Empowerment approach is a carefully developed language arts program, demonstrating outstanding results for Melbourne students'
The programs' systematic, explicit and sequential approach has proven to be of great success, for those with dyslexia, ADHD, autism, and dyscalculia.

The approach teaches students the rules of the English language, through developing phonology (spoken language and letter-sound relationships) and orthography (written language). Once mastered children can spell approximately 80% of English words.

Our method is intrinsically fun for students to master, and includes teaching:
• Manipulation of sounds, including word segmenting, rhyming and blending (phonological and phonemic awareness)
• Handwriting
• Reading and comprehension strategies and objectives, developing fluency and accuracy
• Phonics
• Rules to help students spell, read, and write English words accurately
•Encompassing focuses on high-frequency words, meanings and usage, through etymology

It's time we stopped guessing, and start to feel empowered!
